## Slidikins' Strenuous Skyrim (SSS) Modlist Review (v5.x - Current)

**Slidikins' Strenuous Skyrim** is a well-regarded Wabbajack modlist (~1600+ mods) that serves as a hardcore addon for **The Phoenix Flavour (TPF)**. It transforms Skyrim into a more challenging, survival-oriented experience while staying very close to vanilla in spirit. It emphasizes preparation, resource management, and immersion without radical overhauls or massive new content.

### 1. Core Gameplay Philosophy
**Hardcore Vanilla+ Survival** built on **The Phoenix Flavour** foundation (not Requiem, full EnaiRim, or SimonRim).  
- Philosophy: Make Skyrim harsher and more realistic while remaining lore-friendly and balanced. Strong focus on survival mechanics, limited power growth, and rewarding smart play.  
- Key restrictions: Level cap at 50 (55 perk points total), no skill legendarying, full Survival Mode (eat, sleep, stay warm), equipment degradation, and minimized HUD. Everything rewards preparation and smart resource use.

### 2. Combat System
**Tactical, resource-aware modernized vanilla combat**.  
- Core: **Blade & Blunt** for weighty, stamina-focused fighting with better AI and timing emphasis.  
- Enhancements include Precision, improved animations, directional movement, and survival-integrated mechanics (slower regen, resistances/weaknesses matter).  
- Combat feels dangerous and committed — long fights are punishing. No heavy MCO/For Honor combos; it stays closer to classic Skyrim flow but much more strategic and lethal. Great for all playstyles with emphasis on planning (potions, gear, environment).

### 3. Visuals & Performance
**Solid, performance-friendly visuals** focused on gameplay over pure eye-candy.  
- Uses **Community Shaders** (primary) with optional lighter ENB support. High-quality but reasonable textures, parallax, enhanced lighting/weather, and natural landscapes.  
- **Performance**: Excellent — one of the better optimized lists for its features.  
  - Runs smoothly on mid-range hardware (RTX 3060 or equivalent / 16–32 GB RAM at 1080p/1440p).  
  - Designed for solid framerates rather than maximum graphics. Install size reasonable (~150–250 GB range). Great for players who want visuals without heavy hardware demands.

### 4. Quests & Content
**Sticks close to vanilla with light curated additions**.  
- Vanilla quests and story are preserved and enhanced through better balance and immersion.  
- Limited new quests/lands compared to content-heavy lists — focus remains on improving the core game rather than adding bloat. Some new items, spells, and encounters support survival/roleplay.  
- Exploration feels meaningful due to survival elements and better world reactivity.

### 5. Cities & World
**Enhanced but faithful vanilla world**.  
- Settlements and cities receive quality-of-life and immersion improvements (better NPCs, details, and atmosphere) without massive overhauls like JK's Skyrim or Great Cities.  
- The world feels harsher and more alive through survival systems, dynamic weather, and environmental challenges. Exploration rewards preparedness and knowledge of the land.

### 6. Stability & Difficulty
**Highly stable** thanks to its TPF foundation and careful curation.  
- Excellent patching, active support via Discord, and rare issues for most users.  
- **Difficulty**: **Strenuous and hardcore** by design — early-to-mid game is challenging and demands planning, crafting, and caution. Not as punishing as Requiem but far more demanding than vanilla or light Vanilla+ lists. Rewarding for experienced players who enjoy survival and roleplay.

### Verdict
**Slidikins' Strenuous Skyrim is best suited for players who want a challenging, immersive survival experience that still feels like classic Skyrim.**  
Ideal for veterans seeking a "harder vanilla" with strong roleplaying depth, resource management, and tactical combat on mid-range hardware. More punishing and survival-focused than Nordic Souls or Gate to Sovngarde, far lighter on visuals/content than Nolvus/Wunduniik/LoreRim, and perfect as a step up from pure vanilla without extreme overhauls. A top recommendation in 2026 for those who love preparation, limited power fantasy, and a harsh but fair Tamriel. Highly cohesive and replayable for the right audience.
