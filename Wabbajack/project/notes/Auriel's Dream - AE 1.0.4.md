## Auriel's Dream - AE Modlist Review (Current - ~800-1200+ mods)

**Auriel's Dream - AE** is the Anniversary Edition port/adaptation of the popular **Auriel's Dream** Wabbajack modlist (originally by Kvitekvist, from the FUS team). It is a **visually-focused, high-fantasy Vanilla+ overhaul** with warmer colors and an Oblivion-inspired atmosphere, using Community Shaders for excellent performance. It stays very close to vanilla gameplay while delivering a beautiful, cohesive upgrade.

### 1. Core Gameplay Philosophy
**Pure Visual Remaster / Vanilla+ Foundation** — no heavy framework (not Requiem, EnaiRim, or SimonRim).  
- Philosophy: Enhance the visual experience to next-gen levels while preserving vanilla gameplay, progression, perks, leveling, and balance.  
- Strong high-fantasy theme with warmer lighting and vibrant colors. Optional modules allow adding popular gameplay mods (combat, magic, survival, hard mode). Perfect as a base or standalone for players who want "Skyrim but prettier" without major mechanical changes.

### 2. Combat System
**Vanilla-style combat with light modern touches**.  
- No full MCO/BFCO or heavy animation overhauls by default.  
- Includes VR essentials (or AE equivalents) for smoother feel, better hit reactions, and minor improvements.  
- Combat remains classic Skyrim — accessible and familiar. Easy to enable optional modern combat mods via the installer if desired.

### 3. Visuals & Performance
**Standout high-fantasy visuals** using **Community Shaders**.  
- Warmer color palette, vibrant high-fantasy atmosphere, enhanced lighting/weather, lush landscapes, detailed flora, parallax, and comprehensive texture/NPC overhauls.  
- Feels like a colorful, dreamy remaster with Oblivion vibes.  
- **Performance**: Excellent optimization (one of the better VR-to-AE ports).  
  - Runs well on mid-range hardware (RTX 3060 / 16–32 GB RAM at 1440p). Multiple performance profiles available. Install size manageable compared to heavier lists. Great for both desktop and high-end VR setups.

### 4. Quests & Content
**Sticks very close to vanilla**.  
- No major new quests or lands by default.  
- Vanilla story, factions, and exploration are preserved and enhanced purely through visuals and minor QoL.  
- Optional modules provide ways to add content (e.g., Legacy of the Dragonborn). Excellent clean base for customization.

### 5. Cities & World
**Visually enhanced vanilla world**.  
- Premium textures, lighting, and details applied to cities, settlements, and landscapes without major layout changes.  
- The world feels more vibrant, alive, and high-fantasy while keeping the original structure and immersion intact. Exploration is rewarding through beauty and atmosphere.

### 6. Stability & Difficulty
**Very stable and well-maintained**.  
- Clean patching from the FUS team heritage; minimal issues reported. Active support via Discord.  
- **Difficulty**: Near-vanilla or adjustable via optional hard/survival modes. Approachable for everyone — great for newcomers to modded Skyrim or VR players transitioning to AE.

### Verdict
**Auriel's Dream - AE is best suited for players who want a beautiful, high-fantasy visual overhaul that stays true to vanilla Skyrim on mid-to-high end hardware.**  
It excels as a warm, vibrant, performant remaster (especially strong for those coming from the VR version) and serves as an outstanding customizable base. More accessible and lighter than Nolvus, Wunduniik, or Krentoraan, similar in spirit to Anvil or Eldergleam but with a distinct warmer/Oblivion-inspired aesthetic. Perfect for visual enthusiasts, VR veterans moving to flat AE, or anyone seeking a fresh yet familiar Tamriel experience in 2026 without gameplay overhauls. Highly recommended as a top visual Vanilla+ choice!
