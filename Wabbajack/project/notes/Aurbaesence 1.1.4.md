## Aurbaesence Modlist Review (v1.1.5.x - Current)

**Aurbaesence** (by Zediious) is a heavily curated Wabbajack modlist (~2300+ mods) that evolved from an older version of **The Phoenix Flavour**. It aims to deliver the "**perfect modern Skyrim**" — a near-complete overhaul that keeps the soul of Elder Scrolls while modernizing nearly every system with high-quality additions, tactical gameplay, and immersive enhancements.

### 1. Core Gameplay Philosophy
**Modern Action-RPG overhaul on a Vanilla+ base** (custom framework, not pure Requiem, EnaiRim, or SimonRim).  
- Started as a Phoenix Flavour (Vanilla+) list but evolved far beyond it with extensive custom tweaks and patches.  
- Philosophy: Elevate Skyrim to 2026 standards — everything from combat and animations to mechanics, settlements, quests, creatures, and leveled lists has been touched. Strong focus on third-person play, tactical depth, and making the game feel fresh and replayable after thousands of hours. Highly curated for cohesion and quality.

### 2. Combat System
**Tactical, Souls-like modern action combat** — one of its standout features.  
- Nearly all vanilla combat removed in favor of modern Action-RPG mechanics (inspired by Dark Souls and others).  
- Heavy use of **MCO/ADXP** frameworks, advanced animations, dodging, timing-based combat, poise/stagger systems, and improved AI.  
- Extremely polished third-person focus with fluid movement, conditional animations, and weighty feel. Magic, archery, and melee all benefit from the overhaul. Demands tactical play (positioning, stamina management, preparation) rather than button-mashing.

### 3. Visuals & Performance
**Strong modern visuals** using **Community Shaders** (primary) for excellent efficiency and looks.  
- Full parallax, enhanced textures, detailed flora/landscapes, improved lighting/weather, and comprehensive animation overhauls (movement, idles, dialogue, interactions).  
- Not the absolute most photorealistic (compared to Nolvus), but very cohesive, immersive, and alive.  
- **Performance**: Notably friendly for its size. Multiple profiles (Normal, Performance, Ultra).  
  - Runs well on mid-to-high hardware (RTX 3060/4070+ recommended for smooth 1440p).  
  - Install size ~270 GB mods + downloads (~470 GB total space needed). SSD strongly recommended. Users report good FPS even on demanding setups.

### 4. Quests & Content
**Significant curated content expansion**.  
- Huge amount of new quests, locations, creatures, and gear with enchanted variants.  
- Vanilla quests and locations are overhauled and improved for better pacing and immersion.  
- Expansive leveled lists and new encounters make exploration highly rewarding. Everything feels integrated and lore-friendly while adding modern depth.

### 5. Cities & World
**Enhanced and more alive world**.  
- Settlements and cities receive improvements in detail, population, and interactivity.  
- Worldspace is lush, dynamic, and packed with new content while respecting vanilla structure. Exploration feels vibrant with new creatures, interactables, and atmospheric touches.

### 6. Stability & Difficulty
**Well-patched and stable** for a large curated list, with active Discord support and regular updates.  
- Extensive custom patching helps minimize issues.  
- **Difficulty**: Tactical and challenging (especially early game) due to Souls-like combat and improved mechanics. Rewarding for prepared players — not pure hardcore Requiem, but demands thought and skill. Adjustable elements available.

### Verdict
**Aurbaesence is best suited for players who want a deeply modernized Skyrim with tactical Souls-inspired combat, comprehensive animation overhauls, and a fresh experience while retaining Elder Scrolls soul.**  
Ideal for third-person enthusiasts on mid-to-high end hardware who enjoy curated, cohesive overhauls rather than extreme visuals or kitchen-sink bloat. More modern and action-oriented than Nordic Souls or Gate to Sovngarde, less visually extreme than Nolvus/Wunduniik, and more accessible than pure Requiem lists like LoreRim. A top-tier choice in 2026 for those seeking "Skyrim but better in every way" with excellent replayability and polish.
