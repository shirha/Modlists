## Mages & Vikings Modlist Review (v2.5.x - Current)

**Mages & Vikings** (by SEE YOU L'HATER) is a massive, comprehensive Wabbajack modlist (~3400+ mods) that delivers a **modern, next-gen Skyrim overhaul**. Released in late 2024 and actively updated through 2026, it reimagines nearly every aspect of the game while staying lore-friendly. It has quickly gained a strong reputation as a hidden gem (or underrated favorite) for its stunning visuals, polished combat, and excellent stability.

### 1. Core Gameplay Philosophy
**Modern comprehensive overhaul** with a custom framework (not strictly Requiem, pure SimonRim, or EnaiRim).  
- Heavy focus on breathing new life into Skyrim through curated enhancements to combat, magic, graphics, audio, UI, and mechanics.  
- Philosophy: Create a cohesive 2025/2026 Skyrim experience that feels fresh, immersive, and fun. Strong emphasis on magic (as the name suggests) alongside Viking/Nordic themes, with balanced progression and high replayability. Everything is carefully patched for a smooth, modern feel.

### 2. Combat System
**Modern cinematic action combat** — one of the standout features.  
- Core: **MCO/ADXP** with advanced animations, True Directional Movement, dodging, timing-based mechanics, and improved AI.  
- Combat is skill-focused, weighty, and fluid — timing and positioning matter more than raw stats. Magic feels instant and dynamic (strong mage support with fluid casting). Supports melee, archery, and hybrid builds exceptionally well. Described by players as some of the best Skyrim combat available — responsive, deadly, and satisfying without being overly punishing.

### 3. Visuals & Performance
**Stunning next-gen visuals** with high-end presentation.  
- **ENB**: NAT III (Natural and Atmospheric Tamriel) with tuned presets for beautiful, immersive lighting and atmosphere. Full parallax, high-quality textures, lush flora, detailed landscapes, and comprehensive NPC/animation overhauls.  
- **Performance**: Demanding but optimized.  
  - Recommended: RTX 4070+, 32 GB RAM, modern CPU (runs excellently on RTX 4080/5090 at 1440p/4K).  
  - Install size ~300–350+ GB. Users report buttery-smooth performance and minimal crashes on updated versions, with good frame rates on high-end hardware (DLSS/Frame Generation recommended for max settings).

### 4. Quests & Content
**Significant curated content expansion**.  
- Enhances vanilla quests with better integration while adding many high-quality new quests, locations, gear, spells, and encounters.  
- Strong focus on magic variety and immersive adventures. The world feels packed with things to discover without overwhelming bloat. Excellent for players who want more to do while keeping the core story intact.

### 5. Cities & World
**Enhanced and more immersive world**.  
- Cities and settlements are more detailed, populated, and atmospheric through high-quality overhauls, lighting, and NPC improvements.  
- Landscapes feel lush, varied, and alive with modern flora, weather, and exploration rewards. The "Vikings" theme shines through stronger Nordic atmosphere and immersion.

### 6. Stability & Difficulty
**Excellent stability** — frequently praised as very stable and well-maintained despite the size.  
- Active Discord support and regular updates fix issues quickly. Minimal CTDs reported on current versions.  
- **Difficulty**: Challenging and engaging (timing-based combat makes fights dynamic). More approachable than hardcore Requiem lists but rewarding for prepared players. Great for both mage-focused and warrior playstyles.

### Verdict
**Mages & Vikings is best suited for players with high-end hardware (RTX 4070+) who want a visually spectacular, modern Skyrim with fantastic combat, strong magic support, and a cohesive immersive experience.**  
It stands out as a polished, fun "2025 Skyrim" that many players call one of their favorites — often compared favorably to LoreRim and Nolvus but with its own distinct vibe. More visually and combat-focused than Nordic Souls or Gate to Sovngarde, less simulation-heavy than LoreRim, and more stable/rewarding than some ultra-heavy lists. Perfect for magic enthusiasts, action fans, and explorers seeking a beautiful, replayable Tamriel. Highly recommended as a top-tier choice in 2026!
