## Nolvus v6 (Awakening) Modlist Review (v6.0.20+ - Current)

**Nolvus v6 Awakening** is a massive, fully automated mod collection (3500+ mods) by the Nolvus team. It uses a custom **Nolvus Dashboard installer** (not Wabbajack) and aims to turn Skyrim into a **next-gen, photorealistic AAA experience** while remaining lore-friendly. v6 brings major updates including enhanced MCO combat, Seasons integration, Northern Roads, revamped Legacy of the Dragonborn, and improved stability after beta.

### 1. Core Gameplay Philosophy
**Heavy visual & immersion overhaul with modernized vanilla+ gameplay** — custom framework, not strictly tied to Requiem, EnaiRim, or SimonRim.  
- Focuses on making Skyrim feel like a 2025/2026 remake: photorealistic graphics, expanded content, survival elements, and quality-of-life improvements.  
- Progression is enhanced with new mechanics (stances, survival, better leveling) while keeping the core Elder Scrolls fantasy intact. Strong emphasis on beauty, exploration, and "wow" factor over hardcore RPG simulation.

### 2. Combat System
**Cinematic, modern action combat** — flashy and fun with MCO as the core.  
- **MCO/ADXP** with For Honor-style animations, Valhalla Combat, SCAR, Precision, Ultimate Combat, and a custom **Nolvus Stances Framework** (multiple movesets for weapons, including claws and twinblades).  
- Supports 1st and 3rd person with dodging, parrying, combos, power attacks, and stances. Very stylish and responsive, leaning toward action RPG/JRPG flair rather than pure tactical or Souls-like. Satisfying for melee-focused players; magic and archery also improved.

### 3. Visuals & Performance
**One of the most photorealistic and stunning modlists available** — next-gen visuals are its biggest strength.  
- **ENB**: Cabbage ENB (primary), Kauz ENB, or others via installer. Powered by Natural Atmospheric Tamriel v3, complex parallax, PBR textures, full seasons, and high-end flora/landscapes.  
- Interiors, lighting, weather, and NPC overhauls look breathtaking.  
- **Performance**: Extremely demanding (install size 300–570+ GB depending on variant).  
  - **Ultimate** (full visuals + Open Cities): RTX 3080 Ti+ / 12+ GB VRAM / 32 GB RAM (best at 1440p; 4K needs 4090+).  
  - **Ultra/Redux** variants are lighter for mid-high hardware.  
  - Runs well on high-end PCs with DLSS/Frame Generation; optimized but heavy on draw calls in cities. SSD mandatory.

### 4. Quests & Content
**Significant content expansion** with high-quality additions.  
- Includes **Legacy of the Dragonborn** (heavily revamped with more displays).  
- New lands, dozens of quest mods, expanded vanilla quests, new gear, spells, creatures, and follower options.  
- Survival systems and immersion mods add depth. Everything integrates lore-friendly with a focus on making the world feel alive and full of adventure.

### 5. Cities & World
**Major overhauls for a dense, lived-in world**.  
- Uses **The Great Cities**, custom town/village expansions, Northern Roads, and Open Cities (optional in Ultimate).  
- Cities feel much larger and more detailed with new buildings, NPCs, and seamless transitions. Worldspace is lush, seasonal, and vastly improved with high-end textures and meshes. Exploration is highly rewarding and visually spectacular.

### 6. Stability & Difficulty
**Good stability post-beta** (v6.0.20+ is considered stable with ongoing patches).  
- Custom installer handles most patching; active Discord support. Occasional stutters or rare CTDs in dense areas on max settings.  
- **Difficulty**: Challenging early game but fair and fun overall. Steeper power curve than some lists — you become strong faster. Includes survival elements and adjustable difficulty. Suitable for players wanting an immersive, action-packed experience rather than extreme hardcore punishment.

### Verdict
**Nolvus v6 Awakening is best suited for players with high-end hardware (RTX 30/40/50-series) who want the absolute best visuals and a "Skyrim Remake" feel with flashy combat and tons of content.**  
It excels at photorealism, cinematic gameplay, and expansive world-building, making it more "next-gen AAA" than pure RPG simulation. Compared to LoreRim (more lore-accurate/Requiem-focused), it's flashier and easier to install/modify. Compared to Nordic Souls, it's far more ambitious and demanding. Ideal for screenshot enthusiasts, explorers, and action fans — one of the most impressive visual showcases in Skyrim modding right now. Not for low/mid-range PCs or those seeking lightweight stability.
