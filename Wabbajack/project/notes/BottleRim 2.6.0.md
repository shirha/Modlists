## BottleRim Modlist Review (v2.x / Et' Ada - Current, ~2000+ mods)

**BottleRim** (by dBottle / InTheBottle) is a **modern SimonRim-based Vanilla+** Wabbajack modlist built around **Community Shaders** and full PBR. It delivers a polished, immersive, and performant experience that feels like a refined evolution of Skyrim. Often compared to Nordic Souls, it emphasizes stability, beautiful visuals, satisfying combat, and light survival without extreme overhauls or bloat.

### 1. Core Gameplay Philosophy
**Heavily modified SimonRim base** with light survival.  
- Built on **Adamant perks, Mysticism magic, Blade & Blunt combat, Apothecary**, and other SimonRim tools for balanced, thoughtful progression.  
- Philosophy: "Skyrim, but modern and cohesive." Focus on quality-of-life, immersion, and a living world while staying lore-friendly. Light survival elements add depth without punishing hardcore mechanics. Excellent as both a complete experience and a strong base for customization.

### 2. Combat System
**Modern action with strong third-person focus**.  
- Core: **MCO/ADXP** for third-person (fluid combos, dodging, weighty feel) + **Sigil animations** for first-person.  
- Enhanced with Precision, True Directional Movement, improved AI, and timing elements.  
- Combat is responsive, satisfying, and immersive — more engaging than vanilla while remaining balanced and fun. Great support for melee, magic, and archery playstyles.

### 3. Visuals & Performance
**Excellent full PBR Community Shaders visuals** — the list's biggest strength.  
- Full Community Shaders suite with advanced parallax, high-quality PBR textures, lush landscapes, detailed flora, enhanced lighting/weather, and cohesive NPC overhauls.  
- Clean, natural look that respects vanilla atmosphere while looking next-gen.  
- **Performance**: Notably well-optimized and performant for its size.  
  - Runs smoothly on mid-to-high hardware (RTX 3060/4070 recommended for 1440p).  
  - Strong on mid-range PCs compared to heavier ENB lists. Reasonable install size with excellent frame stability.

### 4. Quests & Content
**Curated vanilla+ with meaningful additions**.  
- Vanilla quests are enhanced with better pacing and integration.  
- Includes select high-quality new quests, gear, creatures, and encounters that feel natural.  
- Good amount of content and exploration rewards without overwhelming the core game. Strong replayability through builds and discovery.

### 5. Cities & World
**Enhanced and more alive world**.  
- Settlements feel populated and detailed through thoughtful improvements.  
- Landscapes are lush, varied, and immersive with PBR upgrades and natural integration. The world feels cohesive and rewarding to explore while staying true to vanilla layout and vibe.

### 6. Stability & Difficulty
**Very stable and well-maintained**.  
- Thorough patching and active support (Discord available). Users praise its reliability and low crash rate.  
- **Difficulty**: Balanced and approachable — more challenging than vanilla due to SimonRim and combat tweaks, but fair and fun. Light survival adds immersion without extreme frustration. Great for both newcomers to modding and veterans.

### Verdict
**BottleRim is best suited for players who want a beautiful, performant, modern Vanilla+ Skyrim with strong SimonRim progression, satisfying MCO combat, and excellent Community Shaders visuals on mid-to-high end hardware.**  
It stands out as a polished, cohesive, and enjoyable list — often described as a strong alternative to Nordic Souls with better PBR visuals and combat. More accessible and stable than heavier lists like Nolvus, Wunduniik, or FILFY, less punishing than LoreRim, and ideal for immersive playthroughs with great longevity. A rising favorite in 2026 for players seeking quality over quantity — highly recommended as a "just works" modern Skyrim experience!
