## FILFY (F.I.L.F.Y.) Modlist Review (v2.x+ - Current, ~3000+ mods)

**F.I.L.F.Y.** (Fantastically Integrated Load-order For You) by AllstaRawR is a massive, **Method-patched** Wabbajack modlist that aims for a fully cohesive, balanced, and content-rich Skyrim experience. It overhauls nearly every system while maintaining strong integration and modularity. The list is known for its "everything but the kitchen sink" approach with excellent patching, Community Shaders focus, and light artistic nudity (no explicit NSFW).

### 1. Core Gameplay Philosophy
**Heavy modular overhaul with strong integration** — custom hybrid approach.  
- Uses "The Method" for deep, line-by-line patching to ensure everything works together.  
- Philosophy: Deliver a fully modernized Skyrim with massive content, balanced systems, and high cohesion. Includes combat, magic, cities, quests, and graphical overhauls while allowing some modularity (optional toggles). Strong emphasis on a lively, expanded world with player freedom.

### 2. Combat System
**Modern MCO action combat**.  
- Core: **MCO/ADXP** with custom animations, dodging, combos, and improved AI.  
- Supporting mods for weapon variety, stances, and reactive enemies.  
- Combat is fluid, cinematic, and engaging across melee, magic, and archery. Well-integrated and tuned for the list's power curve — satisfying without being overly punishing.

### 3. Visuals & Performance
**Strong Community Shaders + PBR visuals**.  
- Full Community Shaders suite with parallax, high-quality textures, enhanced lighting/weather, lush environments, and detailed NPC/creature overhauls.  
- Artistic nudity option (toggleable).  
- **Performance**: Demanding due to size (~650 GB install space required).  
  - Recommended: High-end hardware (RTX 4070+ / 32–64 GB RAM). Heavy on resources — requires a large pagefile and fast NVMe SSD. Multiple graphical adjustment options for performance tuning.

### 4. Quests & Content
**Enormous curated content expansion**.  
- Tons of new quests, continents, weapons, armors, enemies, animals, religions, and locations.  
- Vanilla quests are heavily enhanced alongside many new additions. Alternate starts and modular options give high replayability. The world feels packed and alive.

### 5. Cities & World
**Majorly expanded and overhauled world**.  
- Comprehensive city, environment, and settlement overhauls that make the world denser and more detailed.  
- New continents and fast-travel options. Exploration is highly rewarding with new encounters, audio, and visual variety everywhere.

### 6. Stability & Difficulty
**Good stability thanks to Method patching**, but large size means occasional issues on lower specs.  
- Active community and updates address bugs. Requires proper setup (pagefile especially).  
- **Difficulty**: Balanced and approachable with modern progression. More engaging than vanilla but not extreme hardcore (adjustable via options). Good for players wanting a full-featured experience.

### Verdict
**FILFY is best suited for players with high-end hardware (strong CPU, lots of RAM/VRAM, fast SSD) who want a massive, fully integrated, content-packed Skyrim with modern MCO combat, beautiful Community Shaders visuals, and tons of modular options.**  
It delivers a "complete overhaul" feel with excellent cohesion for its scale. More feature-rich and modular than many kitchen-sink lists, visually strong with CS, and a great choice for those who enjoy variety and exploration. Can be resource-heavy and may need tweaks for optimal performance — not ideal for mid-range PCs or players seeking lightweight lists. A solid, ambitious project in the 2026 Wabbajack scene for enthusiasts wanting "everything integrated."
