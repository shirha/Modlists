## Living Skyrim Modlist Review (v4.x / LS5 in Development - Current)

**Living Skyrim** (by ForgottenGlory) is one of the most popular and long-running Wabbajack modlists (~1100–1500+ mods). It is a **content-heavy immersion overhaul** designed to make Skyrim feel truly alive and packed with things to do. Its core mantra is “breathing life into the world” through population, quests, roleplay, and modern gameplay systems.

### 1. Core Gameplay Philosophy
**Vanilla+ with heavy content and immersion focus** — built primarily on **SimonRim** (Adamant perks, Mysticism magic, etc.) with some custom tweaks.  
- Philosophy: Fill the empty world with NPCs, encounters, quests, and interactive content while keeping it lore-friendly and fun. Strong emphasis on roleplaying, exploration, and a living Tamriel. No extreme deleveling (like Requiem) or pure power fantasy — balanced, rewarding progression that encourages preparation and discovery.

### 2. Combat System
**Modern action-oriented with tactical depth**.  
- Core: **MCO/ADXP** (in LS4) + **Valhalla Combat** for timed blocking, stamina management, and modern feel.  
- Supporting mods: TK Dodge RE, Precision, Know Your Enemy Redux, True Directional Movement.  
- Combat is more fluid, committed, and dangerous than vanilla — enemies dodge, flank, and use cover. Satisfying for melee, archery, and magic. Not full Souls-like but far more engaging and cinematic than base Skyrim.

### 3. Visuals & Performance
**Beautiful and atmospheric visuals** with good optimization.  
- High-quality textures, parallax, enhanced lighting/weather, lush landscapes, and comprehensive NPC overhauls (custom 20,000+ NPC facegen in LS4).  
- ENB options available (bright, immersive presets) alongside Community Shaders.  
- **Performance**: Multiple profiles including a dedicated **Performance mode**. Runs well on mid-range hardware (RTX 3060 / 16–32 GB RAM at 1440p). Install size ~230–350 GB. Excellent balance between looks and playability — one of the more accessible large lists.

### 4. Quests & Content
**One of the strongest content-focused lists** — this is its biggest strength.  
- Massive additions: Legacy of the Dragonborn, Vigilant, Falskaar, Project AHO, tons of new quests, dungeons, followers, and worldspaces.  
- Vanilla quests are expanded with better pacing and integration.  
- Heavy population mods (Immersive College NPCs, Citizens of Tamriel, Immersive Patrols, etc.) make the world feel bustling and dynamic. Exploration is highly rewarding with constant discoveries.

### 5. Cities & World
**Vibrant, populated, and lived-in world**.  
- Cities and towns are significantly more alive with new NPCs, conversations, and details.  
- Uses a mix of high-quality overhauls and population mods rather than extreme layout changes (avoids heavy JK's or Great Cities for better stability/performance).  
- Landscapes feel full and immersive with new encounters, bandit patrols, and interactive elements everywhere.

### 6. Stability & Difficulty
**Very stable and well-maintained** with excellent patching and an active Discord community.  
- Regular updates and a dedicated Performance profile.  
- **Difficulty**: More challenging than vanilla but fair and approachable. Tactical combat and preparation matter, but it's rewarding rather than punishing. Great for roleplay-focused playthroughs with adjustable elements.

### Verdict
**Living Skyrim is best suited for players who want a content-packed, immersive Skyrim that feels truly alive and full of adventure.**  
Perfect for roleplayers, explorers, and those who hated how empty vanilla Skyrim could feel. It offers a fantastic mix of modern combat, beautiful visuals, and endless things to do without extreme hardware demands or hardcore simulation. More content-heavy and approachable than LoreRim, more immersive than pure visual lists like Eldergleam or Nolvus, and a longtime community favorite for good reason. Highly recommended as a first big modlist or for anyone seeking a vibrant, replayable Tamriel in 2026.
