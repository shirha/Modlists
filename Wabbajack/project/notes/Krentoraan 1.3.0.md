## Krentoraan Modlist Review (v1.x - Current)

**Krentoraan** (by Alaxouche, same author as Wunduniik) is a **next-gen visual-only** Wabbajack modlist (~1300+ mods). It is explicitly a trimmed-down, graphics-focused version of Wunduniik, designed as a clean, high-quality base for users who want stunning visuals without heavy gameplay overhauls or bloat. Perfect for building your own custom list on top of a premium foundation.

### 1. Core Gameplay Philosophy
**Pure Visual Remaster / Foundation List** — no rigid gameplay framework (not Requiem, EnaiRim, or SimonRim).  
- Keeps vanilla gameplay systems largely intact while massively upgrading presentation.  
- Philosophy: Deliver breathtaking next-gen looks with excellent stability so users can easily add their preferred combat, quests, or mechanics. Strong emphasis on cohesion, performance balance, and "Skyrim but it looks like a modern AAA remaster."

### 2. Combat System
**Vanilla-style combat** (with light modern touches).  
- No heavy MCO, BFCO, or Souls-like overhauls by default.  
- Uses improved animations, better hit reactions, and minor tweaks for smoother feel while staying true to classic Skyrim combat flow.  
- Ideal for players who want vanilla or lightly modernized combat — easy to add MCO or other frameworks on top thanks to the clean base.

### 3. Visuals & Performance
**Outstanding next-gen visuals** — this is the entire focus of the list.  
- **ENB**: High-quality presets (Silent Horizons 2 and others) with full Lux lighting, complex parallax textures, high-res PBR materials, lush flora/landscapes, detailed NPC overhauls, and atmospheric weather.  
- Inherits the best visual mods from Wunduniik but optimized for a lighter footprint.  
- **Performance**: Significantly better than full Wunduniik.  
  - Runs well on mid-to-high hardware (RTX 3060/4070 recommended for 1440p).  
  - Install size more manageable (~150–250 GB range). Excellent optimization for a visuals-heavy list.

### 4. Quests & Content
**Sticks strictly to vanilla** with minimal additions.  
- No major new quests, lands, or Legacy of the Dragonborn.  
- Designed as a blank canvas — vanilla story and content are preserved and enhanced only through visuals and minor QoL. Perfect base for adding your own quest mods.

### 5. Cities & World
**Visually overhauled vanilla world**.  
- Cities, towns, and landscapes receive premium texture, lighting, and detail upgrades without major layout changes (avoids heavy JK's or Great Cities for stability).  
- The world feels lush, immersive, and next-gen while keeping original structure and vibe intact. Exploration is rewarding purely through beauty and atmosphere.

### 6. Stability & Difficulty
**Excellent stability** — one of its strongest selling points.  
- Thoroughly patched as a visual base; minimal CTDs and conflicts reported. Active Discord support.  
- **Difficulty**: Vanilla-level — approachable for everyone. No hardcore changes, making it ideal for casual or customized playthroughs.

### Verdict
**Krentoraan is best suited for players with mid-to-high end hardware who want a stunning, next-gen visual foundation without gameplay overhauls.**  
It serves as an outstanding, stable base (trimmed from Wunduniik) for building your dream list — add MCO combat, SimonRim, EnaiRim, quests, or whatever you like on top of beautiful graphics. More accessible and lighter than full Wunduniik or Nolvus, a strong alternative to Eldergleam Next-Gen, and perfect for visual enthusiasts or modders who hate manually curating graphics mods. Highly recommended in 2026 as a clean, high-quality "remaster base" list.
