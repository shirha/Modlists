## Anvil Modlist Review (v3.x+ / Current - ~900-1000+ mods)

**Anvil** (originally by Althro, now maintained by Bingus — former author of Ascensio) is a **premium visuals-only** Wabbajack modlist built around **Community Shaders**. It serves as a clean, highly optimized graphical foundation for Skyrim Special Edition (AE), designed as both a ready-to-play beautiful experience and an excellent base for users to build their own custom lists.

### 1. Core Gameplay Philosophy
**Pure Visuals / Foundation List** — no dedicated gameplay framework (not Requiem, EnaiRim, or SimonRim).  
- Keeps vanilla gameplay mechanics almost entirely untouched, with only light QoL and stability improvements.  
- Philosophy: Provide a stunning, cohesive next-gen visual overhaul while staying extremely clean and customizable. Everything is meticulously patched and documented in MO2 notes. Ideal as a "graphics baseline" to layer combat, quests, or progression mods on top.

### 2. Combat System
**Stock vanilla combat** (with minor modern polish).  
- No MCO, BFCO, or heavy animation overhauls included by default.  
- Light improvements to animations, hit reactions, and smoothness for a better-feeling vanilla experience.  
- Perfect blank canvas — extremely easy to add your preferred combat framework (MCO, BFCO, etc.) without conflicts.

### 3. Visuals & Performance
**Excellent modern visuals powered by Community Shaders**.  
- Full Community Shaders suite (parallax, grass, lighting, effects, water, etc.) with high-quality textures, lush landscapes, detailed flora, enhanced weather/lighting, and NPC overhauls.  
- Clean, respectful of vanilla art direction while looking crisp and premium.  
- **Performance**: One of the best-optimized visuals lists.  
  - Runs very well on mid-range hardware (RTX 3060 / 16–32 GB RAM at 1440p).  
  - Significantly lighter than ENB-heavy lists. Install size manageable (~150–250 GB). Multiple profiles available for different performance levels.

### 4. Quests & Content
**Strictly vanilla-focused**.  
- No new quests, lands, or major content additions.  
- Vanilla story, factions, and exploration remain unchanged — enhanced only through superior visuals and atmosphere.  
- Outstanding base for adding your own quest mods (LotD, new lands, etc.).

### 5. Cities & World
**Visually enhanced vanilla world**.  
- Premium textures, lighting, parallax, and details applied to cities, landscapes, and interiors without major layout overhauls.  
- The world feels more alive and immersive purely through graphical fidelity while preserving original structure and feel. Exploration rewards beauty and atmosphere.

### 6. Stability & Difficulty
**Exceptional stability** — frequently praised as extremely clean and reliable.  
- Thorough patching and documentation; minimal issues even when users expand it. Active maintenance.  
- **Difficulty**: Pure vanilla-level — approachable for everyone. No changes to balance or challenge.

### Verdict
**Anvil is best suited for players with mid-to-high end hardware who want a beautiful, performant Community Shaders visual base for Skyrim.**  
It excels as a clean "next-gen remaster" foundation (often compared to or replacing older lists like AVO/ADT) that you can play as-is or heavily customize. More accessible and lighter than full visual powerhouses like Nolvus or Wunduniik, similar in purpose to Krentoraan but with a strong Community Shaders focus. Perfect for modders, visual enthusiasts, or anyone wanting a stable starting point to build their dream list. Highly recommended in 2026 as one of the top pure-graphics options available.
