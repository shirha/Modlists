## Elysium Remastered Modlist Review (Current - ~1800-1900+ mods)

**Elysium Remastered** by TitansBane is a complete rebuild of the original popular Elysium list. It is a massive Wabbajack modlist that **prioritizes stunning visuals** while delivering a fully featured, content-rich experience. It requires the Anniversary Edition upgrade and leans into a modern, enhanced Skyrim with strong EnaiRim gameplay systems.

### 1. Core Gameplay Philosophy
**Visual powerhouse with full EnaiRim suite** — not Requiem or SimonRim.  
- Heavy emphasis on **Enai Siaion’s complete suite** (Ordinator perks, Apocalypse/Odin magic, Andromeda standing stones, Growl, Sacrilege, etc.) for flavorful, powerful, and fun progression.  
- Philosophy: Make Skyrim look and feel like a next-gen remaster while adding depth, new content, and challenge. It balances beauty with expanded role-playing and exploration. Expect a more challenging game than vanilla, but rewarding and replayable.

### 2. Combat System
**Overhauled and in-depth modern combat**.  
- Core mods include **Wildcat** (or similar combat overhauls) for smarter, more aggressive AI, better hit reactions, and balanced difficulty.  
- Enhanced with animation improvements, dodging, power attacks, and tweaks that make combat more engaging and dangerous for the unprepared.  
- Supports both first- and third-person playstyles effectively. Magic, archery, and melee all feel viable and satisfying thanks to EnaiRim integration. More tactical and punishing than vanilla, but not full MCO Souls-like.

### 3. Visuals & Performance
**Primarily visuals-focused** — one of the strongest aspects of the list.  
- High-end ENB (often Cabbage or similar tuned presets), full parallax textures, enhanced lighting/weather, lush landscapes, detailed flora, and premium NPC/character overhauls.  
- Interiors, cities, and environments look breathtaking.  
- **Performance**: Demanding due to the mod count and visual fidelity (install size ~150-250+ GB).  
  - Runs well on mid-to-high hardware (RTX 3060/4070 at 1440p with good FPS using DLSS/optimizations).  
  - High-end PCs (RTX 4080+) handle it beautifully. Optimized but heavy — best with a fast SSD.

### 4. Quests & Content
**Massive content expansion** — a major strength.  
- Includes **Legacy of the Dragonborn**, **Beyond Skyrim - Bruma**, **Wyrmstooth**, **Interesting NPCs**, Forgotten City, and many other high-quality quest/encounter mods.  
- Vanilla quests are enhanced, with tons of new gear, spells, followers, and locations.  
- Hundreds of new additions create a packed world full of adventures while keeping everything lore-friendly.

### 5. Cities & World
**Significantly enhanced and expanded world**.  
- Uses high-quality city overhauls and town expansions (often including elements similar to JK's or custom touches) for more detailed, populated settlements.  
- Lush, immersive landscapes with better textures, lighting, and new points of interest. The world feels vast, alive, and rewarding to explore.

### 6. Stability & Difficulty
**Generally stable** for its size, with good patching and community support via Discord.  
- Occasional issues possible in such a large list, but well-maintained with updates.  
- **Difficulty**: More challenging than vanilla — smarter enemies, better balance, and meaningful preparation required. EnaiRim makes builds feel powerful later on. Suitable for players wanting depth without extreme Requiem-level punishment.

### Verdict
**Elysium Remastered is best suited for players with mid-to-high end hardware who want a visually stunning, content-packed Skyrim experience with EnaiRim's fun and flavorful gameplay systems.**  
It excels as a "kitchen sink" visual and adventure list — beautiful, expansive, and replayable with tons of new quests and role-playing options. More content-heavy and visually ambitious than Nordic Souls or Gate to Sovngarde, less simulation-focused than LoreRim, and more accessible than some ultra-heavy lists like Nolvus. Perfect for veterans or enthusiasts who love exploration, new adventures, and a gorgeous Tamriel without going full hardcore RPG overhaul. A longtime favorite in the Wabbajack community for good reason.
