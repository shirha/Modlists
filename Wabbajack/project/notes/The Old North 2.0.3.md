## The Old North Modlist Review (v2.0.x - Current)

**The Old North** (by granfdad) is a clean, lightweight **Vanilla+** Wabbajack modlist (~300 mods) designed to polish and perfect the original Skyrim experience. It focuses on fixing jank, subtle improvements, and respecting Bethesda’s core design philosophy while delivering a refined, immersive playthrough. It does **not** require the Anniversary Edition upgrade (AE content is removed/reworked).

### 1. Core Gameplay Philosophy
**Pure Vanilla+ perfection** — no heavy frameworks like Requiem, EnaiRim, or SimonRim.  
- Philosophy: "Vanilla Skyrim, perfected." Carefully selected mods fix bugs, improve quality of life, enhance visuals subtly, and refine mechanics without changing the soul of the game. Everything aligns with original Elder Scrolls design — no bloat, no radical overhauls. Ideal for players who love classic Skyrim but want it smoother and prettier.

### 2. Combat System
**Tactical, balanced vanilla-style combat**.  
- Core: **Blade & Blunt** for more thoughtful, resource-aware fighting with attacks of opportunity and harsher penalties.  
- Uses vanilla difficulty scaling (not Blade & Blunt’s built-in difficulty) so it feels only moderately harder than vanilla.  
- Enhanced with **Perks Plus Plus** — a minimalist rework of vanilla perks for better progression and more viable playstyles. Combat feels fair, strategic, and satisfying without modern MCO combos or Souls-like mechanics.

### 3. Visuals & Performance
**Subtle but effective visual polish** using **Community Shaders**.  
- High-quality upscaled textures (**Vanilla Remastered Optimized** + **The New Normal**), improved NPCs (**Kireina**), better weather (**Wander**), and enhanced distant LODs.  
- Stays true to vanilla art direction while looking crisp and modern.  
- **Performance**: Extremely lightweight and well-optimized.  
  - Runs excellently on mid-to-low hardware (even older GPUs with 16 GB RAM).  
  - Very small install size compared to most lists. Great stability and smooth framerates.

### 4. Quests & Content
**Faithful to vanilla with timing tweaks**.  
- No major new quests or lands.  
- Vanilla quests and DLCs are preserved but improved: **Timing Is Everything** delays DLC starts for better pacing (e.g., Dawnguard at level 30+).  
- Focus remains entirely on the base game experience, enhanced through better flow and immersion.

### 5. Cities & World
**Polished vanilla world**.  
- No major city overhauls (avoids JK’s Skyrim or Great Cities).  
- Improvements come through better textures, NPCs, weather, and lighting that make the world feel more alive and cohesive while keeping original layouts and atmosphere intact. Exploration feels fresh and rewarding.

### 6. Stability & Difficulty
**Excellent stability** — one of the cleanest and most reliable lists.  
- Thorough patching and careful curation result in minimal issues.  
- **Difficulty**: Slightly more tactical and challenging than vanilla due to combat tweaks, but very approachable and fair. Great for both new and veteran players.

### Verdict
**The Old North is best suited for players who want the ultimate refined Vanilla+ Skyrim experience — classic gameplay with polished visuals, better combat, and zero bloat on any hardware.**  
It’s perfect for first-time modded players, fans of the original game, or anyone seeking a stable, immersive "Skyrim as it should have been" playthrough. Lighter and more faithful than Nordic Souls, Keizaal, or Legends of the Frost, and a fantastic low-commitment alternative to heavier lists like Nolvus or LoreRim. Highly recommended in 2026 as one of the best pure Vanilla+ options available — clean, beautiful, and endlessly enjoyable.
