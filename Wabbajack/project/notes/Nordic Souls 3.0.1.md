## Nordic Souls Modlist Review (v3.x / 3 PBR - Current)

**Nordic Souls** is a popular Wabbajack modlist (~1200-1500+ mods) by Geborgen, designed as a **quintessential modernized Skyrim experience**. It stays true to the game's Nordic roots and vanilla feel while overhauling visuals, gameplay, and content for a fresh, polished playthrough. It's frequently praised as one of the best "Vanilla+" lists that still feels recognizably like Skyrim.

### 1. Core Gameplay Philosophy
This is built on a **complete SimonRim suite** (not EnaiRim, Requiem, or heavy custom hybrids).  
- SimonRim (Adamant perks, Mysticism magic, Blade & Blunt combat, Apothecary alchemy, etc.) + supporting mods from Delta and others creates a **lightweight but refreshing semi-Vanilla+ overhaul**.  
- Everything is improved and modernized (better progression, fair difficulty, new mechanics) while remaining 100% lore-friendly and recognizably Skyrim.  
- Focus: Streamlined, balanced role-playing with quality-of-life enhancements. No deleveling like Requiem — progression feels natural and rewarding. It adds depth without turning the game into something else (explicitly not "Dark Souls Skyrim").

### 2. Combat System
**Modernized vanilla-style combat with tactical depth** — fluid, responsive, and fun without being overly Souls-like or hack-and-slash.  
- Core: **Blade and Blunt** as the foundation.  
- Key mods: **Precision**, **TK Dodge** (perk-locked behind Agility/light armor), Valravn, SkyTactics, and new animations for both 1st and 3rd person.  
- Combat feels weighty and satisfying with better hit detection, dodging, enemy AI improvements, and revamped bosses (harder dragons/Draugr, but fair). No MCO or heavy animation frameworks — it keeps the classic Skyrim flow while making it more engaging and less spongy. Great for all playstyles (melee, archery, magic).

### 3. Visuals & Performance
**Beautiful, performance-friendly visuals** with strong Nordic atmosphere.  
- **ENB**: Custom/community shaders options (highly optimized; ENB profile available, with Community Shaders alternative for better performance/upscaling).  
- Features: Complex parallax textures, gorgeous grass/landscapes/trees (varied biomes), enhanced lighting/weather, new PBR materials in recent updates, lush yet coherent world.  
- **Performance**: One of the most accessible large lists.  
  - Minimum (60 FPS target): i5-9400 / GTX 1060 / 16 GB RAM.  
  - Recommended: i5-13400 / RTX 3060 Ti / 32 GB RAM (excellent 1440p/4K performance).  
  - ~271 GB install size. Runs smoothly on mid-to-high hardware with minimal stutters after optimization. PBR edition is more demanding but stunning.

### 4. Quests & Content
**Significant but curated content additions** that feel lore-friendly.  
- Vanilla quests are enhanced (better pacing, revamped bosses, new dialogues/NPCs).  
- Adds **tons of new high-quality quests**, lands, gear, and locations that integrate naturally (no overwhelming bloat).  
- New armor/weapon variants, overhauled artifacts, expanded spell variety, and vendor content.  
- Focus on quality over quantity — everything enhances the core Skyrim experience rather than replacing it. Plenty to do with strong replayability through traits and builds.

### 5. Cities & World
**Enhanced vanilla world** with a lighter, more cohesive touch.  
- Cities and settlements feel more alive and detailed without massive overhauls like JK's Skyrim or Great Cities.  
- Worldspace uses high-quality landscape, flora, and texture mods for immersive Nordic environments (lush forests, distinct regions).  
- Exploration is rewarding with natural integration of new content, better encounter zones, and atmospheric improvements. Stays true to vanilla layout and vibe while looking and feeling premium.

### 6. Stability & Difficulty
**Highly stable and polished** — one of the cleanest large Wabbajack lists with excellent patching and ongoing updates.  
- Strong Discord support and regular maintenance.  
- **Difficulty**: More challenging than vanilla but fair and approachable. Better enemy scaling, preparation tools (potions, buffs), and progression. Suitable for first-time modded players or veterans wanting a "what Skyrim should have been" experience. Not hardcore Requiem-level punishing — death feels fair, and builds shine with planning. Includes traits system for build variety.

### Verdict
**Nordic Souls is best suited for players who want a beautiful, modernized Skyrim that still feels like classic Skyrim.**  
Ideal for mid-to-high end PCs, newcomers to heavy modding, or veterans seeking a balanced, lore-faithful experience with SimonRim depth, stunning visuals, and expanded content without extremes. If you love vanilla Skyrim but want it polished for 2025/2026 standards (smooth combat, immersive world, new adventures), this is one of the top recommendations. Less punishing than LoreRim, more accessible and "pure Skyrim" in spirit — highly replayable and endlessly enjoyable.
