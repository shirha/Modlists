## CSVO (Community Shaders Visual Overhaul) Modlist Review (v1.x - Current)

**CSVO** (Community Shaders Visual Overhaul) by dBottle_ (InTheBottle) is a **lightweight, visuals-only Wabbajack modlist** (~300–500 mods) focused entirely on delivering a modern **PBR (Physically Based Rendering)** experience using **Community Shaders**. Released in 2025, it serves as an accessible, clean baseline for next-gen graphics without any gameplay changes — ideal as a foundation for building your own list or enjoying a polished vanilla experience.

### 1. Core Gameplay Philosophy
**Pure Visuals / Moddable Foundation** — no gameplay framework (not Requiem, EnaiRim, SimonRim, or anything else).  
- Philosophy: Provide the best possible Community Shaders + full PBR setup with extensive custom patching for conflict resolution and stability. Strictly visuals, bug fixes, mesh fixes, and QoL for graphics. Designed to be extremely easy to expand — perfect for new users getting into CS or veterans wanting a clean starting point.

### 2. Combat System
**Completely vanilla combat**.  
- No animation overhauls, MCO, BFCO, dodging, or combat tweaks included.  
- Remains 100% stock Skyrim combat (with only minor bug fixes).  
- Excellent blank canvas — users commonly add their preferred combat mods (MCO, BFCO, Blade & Blunt, etc.) on top with minimal conflicts.

### 3. Visuals & Performance
**Strong PBR Community Shaders focus** — the entire purpose of the list.  
- Full Community Shaders suite with advanced PBR textures, parallax, complex grass, water, lighting, effects, and weather integration.  
- Clean, natural, and cohesive look that respects vanilla art direction while looking significantly more modern. Great weather/lighting and detailed environments.  
- **Performance**: Highly optimized and accessible.  
  - Runs excellently on mid-range hardware (RTX 3060 / 16–32 GB RAM at 1440p with solid FPS).  
  - Much lighter than ENB-heavy lists. Install size is reasonable and efficient. Multiple performance-friendly options.

### 4. Quests & Content
**Strictly vanilla**.  
- No new quests, lands, Legacy of the Dragonborn, or major content additions.  
- Vanilla story and exploration are preserved and enhanced only through superior visuals and minor technical fixes. Outstanding as a base for adding your own content mods.

### 5. Cities & World
**Visually upgraded vanilla world**.  
- High-quality PBR textures, meshes, lighting, and details applied across cities, landscapes, interiors, and flora without major layout changes.  
- The world feels premium, immersive, and next-gen while keeping the original structure, density, and feel intact.

### 6. Stability & Difficulty
**Excellent stability** — one of its strongest features.  
- Heavy custom patching for conflicts; very clean and reliable for a visuals list. Active maintenance and easy to update.  
- **Difficulty**: Pure vanilla — completely unchanged and approachable for everyone.

### Verdict
**CSVO is best suited for players who want a clean, performant Community Shaders PBR visual base on mid-to-high end hardware.**  
It’s an ideal starter list for anyone new to Community Shaders or those looking for a stable, lightweight foundation to build upon (add combat, quests, SimonRim/EnaiRim, etc.). More accessible and lighter than heavier visual lists like Anvil, Krentoraan, or Eldergleam, and a strong alternative to pure ENB lists. Perfect for modders, visual enthusiasts, or players wanting "Skyrim but modern and pretty" without gameplay overhauls. Highly recommended in 2026 as one of the best entry-level next-gen visual overhauls.
