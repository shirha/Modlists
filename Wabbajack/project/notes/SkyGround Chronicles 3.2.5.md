## SkyGround Chronicles Modlist Review (v3.x / Current - ~2500+ mods)

**SkyGround Chronicles** (by ItzIvy / Ivy) is a featured Wabbajack modlist that combines **Requiem's hardcore RPG depth** with modern action combat and high-end visuals. It emphasizes survival, exploration, and Legacy of the Dragonborn (LotD) as a central hub, creating a challenging yet rewarding "modern LOTD RPG" experience. It's inspired by lists like LoreRim and Wunduniik but carves its own identity with strong survival focus and BFCO stances.

### 1. Core Gameplay Philosophy
**Requiem-based hardcore survival RPG** with modern enhancements (not pure EnaiRim or SimonRim).  
- Built around **Requiem** for deleveling, meaningful progression, preparation, and immersion.  
- Heavy integration of **Legacy of the Dragonborn** as a core pillar (museum, relics, and collector gameplay).  
- Philosophy: Create a harsh, living Tamriel where survival matters, combat is tactical and stylish, and exploration + collection drive the experience. Strong survival mechanics (hunger, cold, fatigue) layered on Requiem for a demanding but fair challenge.

### 2. Combat System
**Modern action with BFCO stances** — tactical yet cinematic.  
- Core: **BFCO (Attack Behavior Framework)** with full stance system and multiple weapon-type animations (For Honor-inspired movesets).  
- Supporting mods: MCO elements, dodging, precision, improved AI, and survival-integrated combat (stamina management, timing).  
- Combat is weighty, committed, and satisfying with stance switching, combos, and reactive enemies. More modern/action-oriented than pure Requiem while retaining tactical depth. Excellent for melee-focused builds with good magic/archery support.

### 3. Visuals & Performance
**Cutting-edge visuals** pushed to the limit.  
- Supports **ENB** (e.g., Cabbage or similar high-quality presets) and **Community Shaders**. Full parallax, high-res textures, lush landscapes, detailed flora, and atmospheric lighting/weather.  
- **Performance**: Demanding due to visuals + mod count (install ~200–400 GB range).  
  - Recommended: RTX 4070+ / 32 GB RAM for smooth 1440p (optimized profiles available).  
  - Runs on high-end hardware with DLSS/Frame Gen. Recent updates have significantly improved size and optimization.

### 4. Quests & Content
**LOTD-centric with curated expansions**.  
- **Legacy of the Dragonborn** is a major focus, changing gameplay around collecting and museum management.  
- Adds many high-quality quests, new lands, dungeons, gear, and encounters that integrate well with Requiem.  
- Less overwhelming bloat than some kitchen-sink lists — focus is on quality, lore-friendliness, and synergy with survival/Requiem progression. Exploration and relic hunting feel highly rewarding.

### 5. Cities & World
**Enhanced and immersive world** with survival emphasis.  
- Cities and settlements use high-quality overhauls for better population, details, and atmosphere.  
- Landscapes are lush and varied, with dynamic weather, environmental hazards, and survival challenges that make the world feel alive and dangerous. Exploration is central and punishingly rewarding.

### 6. Stability & Difficulty
**Generally stable** with active development and updates.  
- Well-patched for its size; users report good reliability on current versions with occasional early bugs typical of Requiem lists. Active Discord support.  
- **Difficulty**: **Hardcore and strenuous** — early game is very tough (classic Requiem experience). Demands preparation, smart builds, and survival management. Rewarding for dedicated players who enjoy a slow-burn power fantasy. Not suitable for casual runs.

### Verdict
**SkyGround Chronicles is best suited for experienced players with high-end hardware who want a challenging Requiem + LOTD experience with modern BFCO combat, strong survival mechanics, and beautiful visuals.**  
It shines as a hardcore collector/survival RPG list that feels fresh and cohesive — more combat-focused and LOTD-heavy than pure LoreRim, more survival-oriented than many visual powerhouses, and a great choice for those who love preparation, exploration, and museum-building in a dangerous Tamriel. Ideal for fans of punishing yet stylish gameplay in 2026. If you enjoy dying early, growing strong through smart choices, and filling a museum with relics, this list delivers an excellent, immersive adventure.
