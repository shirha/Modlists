## Skyrim Odyssey Modlist Review (v1.1.x - Current)

**Skyrim Odyssey** is a Wabbajack modlist (~1000+ mods) by Soft (SoftSkyrim), released in mid-2025. It focuses on **remastering Skyrim into a next-gen experience while preserving the soul of the original game**. It serves as both a complete, ready-to-play list and an excellent, stable foundation for further customization.

### 1. Core Gameplay Philosophy
**Modern Vanilla+ remaster** — not built on Requiem, full SimonRim, or heavy EnaiRim.  
- Based on a clean vanilla+ foundation with thoughtful modern enhancements.  
- Philosophy: Improve nearly every aspect (graphics, combat, animations, QoL) without changing the core identity of Skyrim. It aims for a fresh yet familiar experience that feels like an official 2025 remaster. Strong emphasis on fun, cohesion, and third-person play while supporting first-person well.

### 2. Combat System
**Modern, engaging, and dual-style combat**.  
- **MCO/ADXP (Attack)** framework for third-person: fluid combos, dodging, parrying, and counter-attacks with modern animations.  
- First-person remains closer to classic Skyrim feel with improvements.  
- Enhanced with better hit reactions, visual impacts, weighty swings, and improved AI. Combat feels dynamic, responsive, and fun — more action-oriented than pure vanilla but not full Souls-like. Great balance for melee, magic, and archery.

### 3. Visuals & Performance
**Strong next-gen visual focus** — one of its biggest strengths.  
- Features **Northern Roads**, **Lux** lighting, **Freaks Floral Fields** grass, high-quality textures, parallax, and comprehensive overhauls.  
- Moody, atmospheric interiors and lush, natural landscapes that make Skyrim feel premium.  
- **Performance**: Well-optimized for its visuals (install size ~170–290 GB).  
  - Runs smoothly on mid-to-high end hardware (RTX 3060/4070+ recommended for 1440p). Good balance between beauty and playability — not as demanding as ultra-heavy lists like Nolvus.

### 4. Quests & Content
**Primarily vanilla-focused with light enhancements**.  
- Vanilla quests and story are preserved and polished (better pacing, integration).  
- Limited major new content additions compared to kitchen-sink lists — focus is on improving the base game.  
- Excellent as a foundation: users often add their own quest mods (e.g., LotD, new lands) thanks to free plugin slots and clean patching.

### 5. Cities & World
**Enhanced vanilla world** with cohesive improvements.  
- Uses Northern Roads and high-end landscape/flora mods for a fresh, detailed look.  
- Cities and settlements feel more alive and visually upgraded through lighting, textures, and details without massive layout overhauls (avoids heavy JK's or Great Cities for stability).  
- Exploration feels rewarding and immersive in a remastered world.

### 6. Stability & Difficulty
**Highly stable and polished** — praised for clean installation and minimal issues.  
- Thorough patching and active support. Excellent for a newer list.  
- **Difficulty**: Slightly more engaging than vanilla due to improved combat and AI, but approachable and fun. No hardcore survival or deleveling — suitable for casual to dedicated players wanting a fresh playthrough.

### Verdict
**Skyrim Odyssey is best suited for players who want a beautiful, modernized Skyrim that still feels like classic Skyrim on mid-to-high end hardware.**  
It excels as a "next-gen remaster" list with strong visuals, responsive combat, and excellent stability while keeping the original soul intact. More accessible and lighter than content-heavy lists like Living Skyrim or Elysium, less punishing than LoreRim or Strenuous Skyrim, and a great alternative to Eldergleam for those seeking solid MCO combat with a vanilla+ base. Perfect for newcomers to big modlists, veterans wanting a clean foundation, or anyone craving a fresh, fun, and gorgeous Tamriel experience in 2026. Highly recommended as one of the strong "remaster-style" options.
