## NGVO (Next Generation Visual Overhaul) Modlist Review (v6.x - Current)

**NGVO** (Next Generation Visual Overhaul) by biggie_boss is a popular **visual-only Wabbajack modlist** (~500–800 mods, very low plugin count under 300 with <40 ESP/ESM). It serves as a premium graphical baseline designed to be easily forked and built upon. Many other lists (e.g., Ghoulified Reality, various Vanilla+ lists) use NGVO as their visual foundation. It delivers stunning next-gen looks while keeping vanilla gameplay completely intact.

### 1. Core Gameplay Philosophy
**Pure Visual Remaster / Forkable Foundation** — no gameplay framework at all.  
- Philosophy: Provide the absolute best modern visuals in a clean, stable, low-conflict package. Everything else (combat, progression, quests) remains 100% vanilla or easily customizable.  
- Strong focus on moddability — over 210 free ESP slots, Stock Game technology, and excellent documentation for adding your own mods.

### 2. Combat System
**Completely vanilla combat**.  
- No MCO, BFCO, Wildcat, Blade & Blunt, or animation overhauls included.  
- Only minor bug fixes and smoothness improvements.  
- Perfect blank canvas — extremely easy to add any combat system (MCO, BFCO, SimonRim, etc.) on top with minimal patching needed.

### 3. Visuals & Performance
**Next-gen visual showcase** — the entire purpose of the list.  
- **ENB**: Cabbage ENB (primary with custom tweaks) + Terrain Helper support.  
- Key mods: Vanaheimr Landscapes, Nature of the Wild Lands, Freak's Floral Meadows, high-end parallax/PBR textures, Lux lighting, enhanced weather, detailed flora, NPC overhauls, and premium audio/UI improvements.  
- Vibrant yet natural atmosphere with photorealistic potential.  
- **Performance**: Well-optimized for a visuals list.  
  - Runs smoothly on mid-to-high hardware (RTX 3060/4070+ recommended for 1440p). Multiple performance profiles. Reasonable install size compared to heavier lists.

### 4. Quests & Content
**Strictly vanilla**.  
- No new quests, lands, Legacy of the Dragonborn, or major content additions.  
- Designed purely as a visual upgrade — vanilla story and exploration enhanced only through breathtaking graphics and minor QoL/bug fixes.

### 5. Cities & World
**Visually overhauled vanilla world**.  
- Premium textures, lighting, parallax, and details applied across cities, landscapes, interiors, and environments without changing layouts or adding major population mods.  
- The world feels lush, immersive, and next-gen while preserving the original structure and feel perfectly.

### 6. Stability & Difficulty
**Excellent stability** — one of the cleanest visual lists available.  
- Thorough patching, low conflict rate, and active maintenance. Very reliable even after adding mods.  
- **Difficulty**: Pure vanilla — completely unchanged and approachable for all players.

### Verdict
**NGVO is best suited for players with mid-to-high end hardware who want a stunning, next-gen visual foundation that is easy to customize and fork.**  
It excels as a clean, high-quality baseline for building your dream list or playing pure vanilla with breathtaking graphics. More optimized and moddable than heavier visual lists like Nolvus or Wunduniik, similar in purpose to Anvil, Krentoraan, or CSVO but with a strong ENB focus and excellent community adoption. Perfect for visual enthusiasts, modders, or anyone wanting "Skyrim but it looks like a 2026 remaster" without gameplay changes. A top recommendation in 2026 for anyone starting a new visual setup!
