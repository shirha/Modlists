## Eldergleam Next-Gen Modlist Review (v4.x / NG - Current)

**Eldergleam Next-Gen** is a Wabbajack modlist (1000–1600+ mods) by Capt. Panda. It serves as a **premium visual foundation** built on **The Phoenix Flavour (SME)** base. It transforms vanilla Skyrim into a stunning next-gen experience with photorealistic textures and modern animations while keeping gameplay close to classic Skyrim. It's designed as both a ready-to-play list and an excellent base for further customization.

### 1. Core Gameplay Philosophy
**Vanilla+ with heavy visual focus** — not built on Requiem, full SimonRim, or EnaiRim.  
- Core is **SME (The Phoenix Flavour)** for a clean, lore-friendly vanilla+ foundation with quality-of-life tweaks.  
- Philosophy: Deliver breathtaking next-gen visuals and smoother animations while preserving the original Skyrim feel and progression. Minimal gameplay overhauls — perfect for players who want "Skyrim but it looks like a 2025 AAA remaster." Highly customizable with tons of free plugin slots (~100-150 used).

### 2. Combat System
**Modernized vanilla combat with MCO enhancements**.  
- **MCO/ADXP** as the core (Modern Combat Overhaul) with Elden Rim movesets, SCAR for NPC reactions, and Precision.  
- Fluid third-person combos, better hit detection, and improved animations without going full Souls-like or overly punishing.  
- Keeps the classic Skyrim flow (dodging, blocking, power attacks) but makes it more responsive and cinematic. Magic and archery remain strong and vanilla-feeling. Great middle ground — engaging without extreme changes.

### 3. Visuals & Performance
**One of the strongest pure visual showcases** — photorealistic and cinematic.  
- **ENB**: Cabbage ENB (primary) with options for lighter presets or Community Shaders. Full parallax textures (2K-4K), complex landscapes, NAT III weather, Lux lighting.  
- Lush grass/flora, detailed mountains, vibrant yet natural Nordic atmosphere.  
- **Performance**: Demanding but optimized with profiles (Normal / Performance / Ultra).  
  - Runs well on mid-to-high hardware: RTX 3060/4070 at 1440p (50-70+ FPS with tweaks/Frame Generation).  
  - High-end (RTX 4080/5090) handles 4K beautifully. Install size ~150-250 GB. Excellent stability and frame pacing for a visuals-heavy list.

### 4. Quests & Content
**Sticks very close to vanilla** — no major new content.  
- Focus is entirely on enhancing the base game visually and through animations.  
- No new quests, lands, or Legacy of the Dragonborn-style additions. Vanilla quests, factions, and story remain untouched.  
- Ideal as a base — users commonly add their own quest mods (e.g., Gray Cowl, Vigilant) without major conflicts.

### 5. Cities & World
**Enhanced vanilla world** with no major layout changes.  
- Uses high-quality texture/mesh overhauls for cities, landscapes, and interiors without JK's Skyrim or Great Cities overhauls.  
- Settlements feel more detailed and alive through better textures, lighting, and NPC visuals. World exploration is rewarding due to parallax and atmospheric improvements while keeping the original layout and vibe intact.

### 6. Stability & Difficulty
**Excellent stability** — frequently praised as one of the cleanest and most stable large visual lists.  
- Thoroughly patched; minimal CTDs reported by users. Active Discord support.  
- **Difficulty**: Vanilla-level with slight modern tweaks. Approachable for all players — no hardcore Requiem punishment. Perfect for casual or first-time heavy-modded playthroughs, with room to add difficulty mods if desired.

### Verdict
**Eldergleam Next-Gen is best suited for players who want a breathtakingly beautiful Skyrim that still feels like classic Skyrim.**  
It's the ideal "next-gen remaster" list for mid-to-high end PCs, visual enthusiasts, and those who love customizing their own experience. More accessible and stable than heavier lists like Nolvus or Wunduniik, less gameplay-focused than LoreRim or Nordic Souls, and far more visually impressive than pure vanilla+. Perfect starter for newcomers to modding or veterans building their dream list. If you prioritize looks, smoothness, and flexibility over massive content or hardcore RPG systems, this is one of the top choices in 2026.
