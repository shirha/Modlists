## Tempus Maledictum Modlist Review (v7.x / v8.x - Current)

**Tempus Maledictum** (often called "Tempus") is a popular "kitchen sink" Wabbajack modlist (1400–2000+ mods) by LivelyDismay. It prioritizes **pure fun** above all else — a vibrant power fantasy experience packed with new content, while staying mostly lore-friendly. It adds tons of quests, gear, NPCs, and locations without turning Skyrim into a completely different game.

### 1. Core Gameplay Philosophy
**Kitchen sink / Power Fantasy** — not built on a strict framework like pure Requiem, SimonRim, or EnaiRim.  
- Heavy **EnaiRim suite** (Ordinator perks, Apocalypse/Odin magic, Andromeda stones, Growl, etc.) as the backbone for flavorful, powerful progression.  
- Some older versions had Requiem influences, but current versions lean toward accessible, fun, and rewarding gameplay.  
- Philosophy: "If it's fun and I like it, it goes in." Massive focus on new content, exploration, role-playing freedom, and feeling like a powerful Dragonborn. Excellent for players who want a packed, replayable world without extreme hardcore restrictions.

### 2. Combat System
**Modernized vanilla-style with added depth and flair** — responsive and combo-friendly, not full Souls-like or heavy MCO spam.  
- Uses **BFCO** (in recent versions) or similar animation frameworks with TK Dodge, Valravn/Blade & Blunt elements, and custom behaviors.  
- Combat supports dodging, timed blocking, power attacks, and weapon-specific combos/movesets. More engaging than vanilla while keeping the classic Skyrim flow.  
- Magic and archery feel strong thanks to EnaiRim. Overall, it's fun, flashy when desired, and accessible rather than punishingly tactical.

### 3. Visuals & Performance
**Solid, vibrant visuals** with good atmosphere and a focus on prettiness without maximum photorealism.  
- **ENB**: Culminated ENB or similar community presets (bright, colorful, and immersive).  
- Features lush forests, enhanced textures, lighting, and NPC overhauls that make the world feel alive and inviting.  
- **Performance**: Surprisingly well-optimized for its size.  
  - Runs acceptably on mid-range hardware (e.g., GTX 1660 Ti / RTX 3060 with 16–32 GB RAM).  
  - Best on mid-to-high end PCs (RTX 3070+ recommended for smooth 1440p). Install size ~200+ GB. SSD required to minimize stutters. Users report it performs better than expected for the mod count, with tweaks available for lower specs.

### 4. Quests & Content
**Massive content focus** — one of its biggest strengths.  
- Tons of new quests, lands, NPCs, weapons, armors, and locations that expand the world naturally.  
- Includes **Legacy of the Dragonborn (LotD)** and many high-quality quest mods.  
- Vanilla quests are enhanced with better pacing, dialogues, and integration. Heavy emphasis on exploration and discovery. Everything feels like a power fantasy playground with new adventures around every corner.

### 5. Cities & World
**Enhanced and populated world** with a lighter touch on overhauls.  
- Cities feel more alive with added details, NPCs, and new buildings without massive layout changes like JK's or Great Cities.  
- Worldspace is lush, varied, and packed with new encounters, interactables, and hidden gems. Exploration is highly rewarding and central to the experience.

### 6. Stability & Difficulty
**Excellent stability** — frequently praised as one of the most stable large lists despite the mod count.  
- Thoroughly patched and maintained with active support. Rare issues for most users.  
- **Difficulty**: Power fantasy leaning — challenging but fair and rewarding. You grow into a legend faster than in Requiem lists. Adjustable via MCMs and EnaiRim tools. Great for both casual and dedicated players who want fun without constant frustration.

### Verdict
**Tempus Maledictum is best suited for players who want a fun, content-packed power fantasy Skyrim full of new adventures, strong EnaiRim progression, and solid modern gameplay without extreme hardcore mechanics or ultra-demanding visuals.**  
It's the perfect "kitchen sink" list for those who love exploring, collecting, and feeling powerful while still recognizing vanilla Skyrim's soul. More accessible and content-heavy than LoreRim, less simulation-focused than Nolvus, and more adventurous than Nordic Souls. Highly recommended for veterans and newcomers alike who prioritize enjoyment and replayability on mid-to-high end hardware. One of the most beloved large lists for good reason — pure fun in Tamriel.
