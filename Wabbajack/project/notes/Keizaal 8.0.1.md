## Keizaal Modlist Review (v8.x - Current)

**Keizaal** ("A Simple Modlist" by Tate Taylor / Pierre Despereaux) is a long-running, highly respected Wabbajack modlist (~700–900+ mods) that delivers a **pure Vanilla+ experience**. It enhances and expands Skyrim while staying extremely faithful to Bethesda’s original 2011 vision, with strong emphasis on immersion, stability, and lore-friendliness. Often described as one of the best "feels like Skyrim, but better" lists.

### 1. Core Gameplay Philosophy
**Vanilla+ with light SimonRim influences** — not Requiem, full EnaiRim, or heavy custom overhauls.  
- Philosophy: Everything should feel like it always belonged in the base game. No complicated new systems, no immersion-breaking mechanics. Strong focus on lore accuracy, consistency, and longevity.  
- Uses **SimonRim suite** (Adamant, Mysticism, Blade & Blunt, etc.) in a restrained way for balanced, thoughtful progression. Light survival and QoL additions integrate seamlessly. Perfect for players who want enhanced vanilla without extremes.

### 2. Combat System
**Modernized vanilla-style tactical combat**.  
- Core: **Blade & Blunt** for weighty, stamina-focused, and more dangerous fights.  
- Enhanced with improved animations, better AI, Precision, and directional elements. Combat feels slower, more committed, and strategic — enemies are smarter and fights require thought, but it stays true to classic Skyrim flow. No heavy MCO or Souls-like combos; accessible yet engaging for all playstyles.

### 3. Visuals & Performance
**Beautiful, cohesive visuals** using **Community Shaders** (primary) for efficiency.  
- High-quality textures, parallax, enhanced lighting/weather, lush but natural landscapes, and detailed NPC overhauls. Respectful of vanilla art direction while looking crisp and immersive.  
- **Performance**: Excellent — one of the lighter large lists.  
  - Runs very well on mid-range hardware (RTX 3060 / 16–32 GB RAM at 1080p/1440p).  
  - Install size ~115 GB (plus downloads). Great optimization and stability make it accessible for many PCs.

### 4. Quests & Content
**Curated vanilla expansions** with meaningful additions.  
- Vanilla quests receive polish and better integration.  
- Includes high-quality additions like **Wyrmstooth**, **Beyond Bruma**, Lawbringer, and select new encounters/gear that feel native.  
- Focus on quality and lore-friendliness — adds replayability and exploration rewards without bloat or kitchen-sink overload.

### 5. Cities & World
**Gently enhanced vanilla world**.  
- Uses targeted improvements (e.g., Cities of the North elements) for more unique and detailed settlements without massive overhauls.  
- Landscapes feel alive and immersive through better textures, flora, and atmosphere. Exploration remains rewarding and true to the original layout and vibe.

### 6. Stability & Difficulty
**Exceptional stability** — frequently praised as one of the cleanest and most reliable lists.  
- Thorough patching, long-term support, and minimal issues even in long playthroughs. Active community.  
- **Difficulty**: More challenging than vanilla due to smarter combat and light survival elements, but fair and approachable. Great for both newcomers to modding and veterans seeking a polished, immersive experience.

### Verdict
**Keizaal is best suited for players who want a beautiful, stable, lore-faithful Vanilla+ Skyrim that feels like a true remaster of the original game.**  
It’s ideal for mid-range hardware, immersion lovers, and those who prefer "Skyrim but enhanced" over heavy overhauls or power fantasies. More accessible and lighter than Living Skyrim, Nordic Souls, or Gate to Sovngarde, less demanding than visual beasts like Nolvus or Wunduniik, and far less punishing than LoreRim. A longtime community favorite in 2026 for its cohesion, replayability, and "it just works" reliability — perfect as a first big modlist or for long-term playthroughs.
