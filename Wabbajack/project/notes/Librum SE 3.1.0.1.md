## Librum SE Modlist Review (v3.x / Current)

**Librum SE** (by the Scenic Route team) is a **gritty, low-magic hardcore survival** Wabbajack modlist (~1500–2000+ mods) designed as an uncompromising old-school RPG experience. It draws heavy inspiration from classic Elder Scrolls titles like Daggerfall, turning Skyrim into a dangerous, low-fantasy world where preparation, survival, and smart progression are essential.

### 1. Core Gameplay Philosophy
**Hardcore low-magic survival RPG**.  
- Custom hybrid framework with heavy survival, limited magic, and realistic progression.  
- Philosophy: "A gritty, low-magic, hardcore survival-adventure experience with an uncompromising vision." Magic is rare and must be discovered/crafted; combat is deadly; the world feels harsh and consequential. Strong focus on immersion, roleplaying, and earning your power slowly. Not a power fantasy — expect to struggle and die often.

### 2. Combat System
**Tactical, dangerous, and punishing**.  
- Core: **Blade & Blunt** + **Vigor - Enhanced Combat** for stamina-focused, dynamic fighting.  
- Supporting mods: Mortal Enemies, Skyrim Revamped - Complete Enemy Overhaul, Know Your Enemy, and improved AI.  
- Combat is weighty, realistic, and high-stakes — every swing costs stamina, enemies are smarter and more lethal, and positioning/timing matter. No flashy MCO combos or Souls-like flair by default — it feels grounded and brutal. Magic is limited and requires research/crafting.

### 3. Visuals & Performance
**Atmospheric gritty visuals** with good optimization.  
- Uses **Community Shaders** (in newer updates) alongside tuned ENB options (Ominous ENB default with performance presets). Darker, moodier lighting/weather, enhanced textures, and immersive effects that support the low-fantasy tone.  
- **Performance**: Relatively accessible for a large list.  
  - Runs well on mid-range hardware (RTX 3060 / 16–32 GB RAM at 1440p with tweaks). Multiple ENB/performance profiles available. Install size manageable compared to ultra-heavy visual lists.

### 4. Quests & Content
**Significant curated content with low-magic focus**.  
- Hundreds of new quests, locations, creatures, and adventures that expand the world while fitting the gritty tone.  
- Vanilla quests are heavily reworked for better pacing and integration. Emphasis on exploration, crafting, and discovery rather than hand-holding. New systems for spell research and realistic progression.

### 5. Cities & World
**Harsh, immersive, and lived-in world**.  
- Enhanced settlements with better atmosphere and details that reinforce the survival theme.  
- Landscapes feel cold, dangerous, and realistic with dynamic weather, wildlife behaviors, and environmental hazards. Exploration is risky and rewarding — the world feels vast and unforgiving.

### 6. Stability & Difficulty
**Stable with dedicated support**.  
- Well-patched by the Scenic Route team with active Discord and wiki. Regular updates address balance and bugs.  
- **Difficulty**: **Extremely hardcore** — early game is brutal, magic is scarce, fast travel is limited, and death has consequences. Designed for players who enjoy challenge, preparation, and slow-burn progression. Adjustable elements exist but the default is punishing.

### Verdict
**Librum SE is best suited for hardcore survival enthusiasts who want a gritty, low-magic Skyrim that feels like a true classic Elder Scrolls RPG on mid-to-high end hardware.**  
It delivers a dark, immersive, and challenging experience where every decision matters — perfect for fans of Requiem-style difficulty but with more modern polish and content. More punishing and survival-focused than Nordic Souls or Keizaal, less visually extreme than Nolvus/Wunduniik, and a strong alternative to LoreRim for those seeking low-fantasy hardship. Highly recommended in 2026 for dedicated players who enjoy dying, learning, and eventually mastering a harsh Tamriel. Check the official wiki and Discord for the full Player's Handbook!
