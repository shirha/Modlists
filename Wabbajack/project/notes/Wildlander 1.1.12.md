## Wildlander Modlist Review (v1.1.x - Current)

**Wildlander** is a long-standing, highly regarded **Requiem-based hardcore survival/roleplaying** Wabbajack modlist (~500–1000+ mods). It transforms Skyrim into a gritty, immersive, and punishing world where every decision matters. Often called a spiritual successor to older lists like Ultimate Skyrim, it emphasizes realism, preparation, and meaningful progression in a dangerous Tamriel.

### 1. Core Gameplay Philosophy
**Hardcore Requiem survival RPG**.  
- Built entirely around **Requiem** as the foundational overhaul (deleveling the world, removing hand-holding, and making everything feel consequential).  
- Philosophy: A "fantasy life simulator" of beauty and brutality. You start weak and must survive through smart choices, crafting, exploration, and roleplaying. Heavy focus on immersion, survival mechanics (hunger, cold, fatigue), and earning your power slowly. Not a power fantasy — death is common and educational.

### 2. Combat System
**Tactical, realistic, and punishing**.  
- Core: **Requiem** + **Wildcat** and **Mortal Enemies** for smarter, more aggressive AI and weighty combat.  
- Enhanced with hit reactions, stamina management, and better enemy behaviors. No heavy MCO or flashy combo systems — combat feels grounded, dangerous, and committed. Every fight requires preparation, positioning, and resource awareness. Magic and stealth are viable but demand careful build-crafting.

### 3. Visuals & Performance
**Atmospheric and optimized visuals**.  
- Uses high-quality but reasonable textures, enhanced lighting/weather, natural landscapes, and immersive effects that support the gritty survival tone.  
- **Performance**: Notably lightweight and friendly compared to modern visual powerhouses.  
  - Runs well on mid-range hardware (RTX 3060 / 16–32 GB RAM at 1080p/1440p). Excellent optimization makes it accessible for many PCs.

### 4. Quests & Content
**Vanilla+ with curated immersive additions**.  
- Vanilla quests are preserved and enhanced through better integration and realism.  
- Focuses on quality roleplay tools, new encounters, crafting systems, and exploration rewards rather than massive new quest mods or lands. The world feels reactive and alive without bloat.

### 5. Cities & World
**Harsh, lived-in, and immersive world**.  
- Settlements feel more realistic and populated through subtle improvements.  
- Landscapes are beautiful yet dangerous, with dynamic weather, wildlife, and environmental hazards that reinforce survival gameplay. Exploration is central and highly rewarding (or punishing).

### 6. Stability & Difficulty
**Very stable and polished** for a Requiem list, with strong community support.  
- Well-maintained with regular compatibility updates.  
- **Difficulty**: **Extremely hardcore** — early game is brutal and demands patience, planning, and roleplaying. One of the most punishing yet fair survival lists. Great for dedicated RP/survival fans, but not beginner-friendly.

### Verdict
**Wildlander is best suited for experienced players who want a deeply immersive, hardcore Requiem survival experience with strong roleplaying and a dangerous, beautiful Tamriel.**  
It excels as a refined "old-school" RPG simulator — punishing but fair, with excellent atmosphere and longevity. More survival/RP-focused and lighter on visuals than LoreRim or modern lists like Apostasy, less flashy than power fantasy options, and a classic favorite for those who enjoy slow-burn progression and realistic hardship. Still highly recommended in 2026 for fans of challenging, lore-friendly immersion, though some newer lists offer more frequent updates.
