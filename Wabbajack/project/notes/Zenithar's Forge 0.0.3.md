@# Zenithar's Forge Modlist Review (Beta 1 / Current)

**Zenithar's Forge** (by ForgottenGlory, creator of Living Skyrim) is a **hardcore roleplaying-focused** Wabbajack modlist (~700+ mods) designed as the ultimate immersive simulation. It transforms Skyrim into a deep, systems-heavy sandbox where you can live as a humble craftsman, chef, merchant, or adventurer — starting as a nobody and forging your own path through survival, crafting, and meaningful choices.

### 1. Core Gameplay Philosophy
**Ultimate Roleplaying Simulation** — heavily customized with survival, crafting, and simulation elements.  
- Not built on Requiem, full EnaiRim, or SimonRim — instead a unique custom framework emphasizing deep systems and player freedom.  
- Philosophy: You are not the chosen one at the start. Become a blacksmith, alchemist, chef, trader, or warrior through realistic progression. Strong focus on survival, economy, crafting loops, and immersive roleplay. "Losing is fun" — failure and slow growth are part of the experience.

### 2. Combat System
**Tactical, dangerous, and punishing**.  
- Combat is overhauled to be lethal and realistic — even basic enemies like wolves pose a serious threat early on.  
- Improved AI, timing-based mechanics, stamina/resource management, and integration with survival systems.  
- No flashy MCO power fantasy — fights feel grounded, committed, and high-stakes. Magic and crafting play major supporting roles. Every encounter demands preparation and smart play.

### 3. Visuals & Performance
**Solid, immersive visuals** focused on atmosphere over maximum photorealism.  
- Enhanced textures, lighting/weather, landscapes, and NPC visuals that support the lived-in, realistic feel.  
- **Performance**: Relatively accessible for its depth (better than massive 3000+ mod lists).  
  - Runs on mid-range hardware (RTX 3060 / 16–32 GB RAM recommended).  
  - Install size is manageable. Optimized for long play sessions with many simulation systems active.

### 4. Quests & Content
**Roleplay-driven with strong vanilla enhancements**.  
- Vanilla quests exist but are experienced through a realistic lens (you start weak and must earn power).  
- Heavy emphasis on crafting professions (blacksmithing, alchemy, cooking, etc.), economy, and player-created goals.  
- New systems, locations, and mechanics support living in Skyrim as a citizen rather than rushing the main story. Exploration and daily life feel meaningful.

### 5. Cities & World
**Lived-in and reactive world**.  
- Settlements feel populated and functional with economy, NPC schedules, and immersive details.  
- The world reacts to your roleplay choices — trade, crafting, survival, and reputation matter. Landscapes and environments support a harsh, realistic Nordic survival fantasy.

### 6. Stability & Difficulty
**Stable for a systems-heavy list** with active development (from the Living Skyrim team).  
- Thorough patching, though as a beta it may have occasional quirks. Good Discord support.  
- **Difficulty**: **Extremely hardcore** — one dedicated "Zenithar's Forge" difficulty. Early game is brutal; everything can kill you. Designed for dedicated roleplayers who enjoy slow progression, management sim elements, and high challenge. Not for casual playthroughs.

### Verdict
**Zenithar's Forge is best suited for hardcore roleplaying enthusiasts who want a deep, immersive simulation Skyrim where you can live as a craftsman, chef, or adventurer in a dangerous world.**  
It shines as a unique "live another life" experience with strong survival, crafting, and economy systems — very different from power fantasy or visual showcase lists. More simulation-focused and punishing than Living Skyrim, far less flashy than Nolvus or LoreRim, and perfect for players who enjoy slow-burn progression and meaningful daily life in Tamriel. Currently in Beta, so expect ongoing polish, but it delivers a fresh and rewarding take on Skyrim roleplay in 2026. Highly recommended for the right audience!
