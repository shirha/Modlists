## Fahluaan Modlist Review (v2.x / Current - ~2300-3000+ mods)

**Fahluaan** (by Aljo / Oghma-Infinium, also creator of Arisen and Ascencio) is a meticulously balanced, lore-faithful Wabbajack modlist that modernizes Skyrim's combat and gameplay while preserving the original Elder Scrolls vision. It stands out as a "Skyrim 2.0" experience — cohesive, stable, and highly praised by players for feeling like a true evolution of the base game.

### 1. Core Gameplay Philosophy
**Well-balanced SimonRim foundation** with modern enhancements.  
- Built primarily on the **full SimonRim suite** (Adamant perks, Mysticism magic, Blade & Blunt, Apothecary, etc.) for thoughtful, fair progression.  
- Philosophy: Bring Skyrim into the modern age through improved combat, animations, QoL, and curated content without breaking lore or turning it into a different game. Everything is handpicked and patched for seamless integration — no bloat, strong emphasis on balance and replayability.

### 2. Combat System
**Modern action-oriented combat** — a major highlight.  
- Core: **MCO/ADXP** framework with updated animations, True Directional Movement, dodging, and improved hit reactions.  
- Enhanced by Blade & Blunt principles for weighty, tactical feel with better AI and timing emphasis.  
- Fluid, responsive, and satisfying for melee, magic, and archery. More engaging and cinematic than vanilla while staying true to Skyrim's roots — not full Souls-like or overly flashy.

### 3. Visuals & Performance
**Strong, cohesive visuals** with excellent optimization.  
- Uses high-quality textures, parallax, enhanced lighting/weather (often with Northern Roads integration), lush landscapes, and detailed NPC/animation overhauls.  
- ENB and Community Shaders options available for beautiful yet performant results.  
- **Performance**: Surprisingly good for the mod count — frequently praised for running better than expected.  
  - Solid on mid-to-high hardware (RTX 3070/3080+ recommended for smooth 1440p). Users report stable 60+ FPS with minimal issues on capable rigs. Install size substantial but well-optimized.

### 4. Quests & Content
**Curated content expansion** that integrates seamlessly.  
- Handpicked new quests, lands, gear, spells, and encounters that feel like they belong in vanilla Skyrim.  
- Vanilla quests are enhanced with better pacing and integration. Focus on quality over quantity — adds meaningful adventures and replayability without overwhelming the core experience.

### 5. Cities & World
**Enhanced and immersive world** with natural improvements.  
- Uses Northern Roads and high-quality landscape/flora mods for varied, beautiful biomes.  
- Cities and settlements receive thoughtful details and population tweaks while respecting vanilla layouts. Exploration feels fresh, rewarding, and lore-accurate with dynamic elements.

### 6. Stability & Difficulty
**Excellent stability** — one of the strongest points according to community feedback.  
- Thoroughly patched and maintained with active support (Discord is responsive). Minimal crashes reported on current versions.  
- **Difficulty**: Balanced and fair — more challenging than vanilla due to improved combat and mechanics, but approachable and rewarding. Great for players wanting depth without extreme hardcore punishment (not Requiem-level).

### Verdict
**Fahluaan is best suited for players who want a polished, lore-friendly "Skyrim 2.0" experience with modern combat, strong SimonRim progression, beautiful visuals, and excellent stability on mid-to-high end hardware.**  
It delivers a cohesive, balanced, and highly enjoyable playthrough that feels like an official evolution of the game. More modern and accessible than hardcore lists like LoreRim or SkyGround Chronicles, less visually extreme than Nolvus/Wunduniik, and a step above many Vanilla+ lists in depth and polish. Frequently called one of the best overall lists by veterans — perfect for long-term playthroughs in 2026. Highly recommended if you value balance, immersion, and "it just works" reliability.
